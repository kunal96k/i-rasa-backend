Usage & Care Guidelines

1. Proper Storage (The Golden Rule)

  • Store in a Cool, Dark Area: Always store your perfume in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, heat, and humidity.
  • Avoid keeping it in the bathroom or near windows, as fluctuating temperatures can break down the delicate fragrance notes over time. A drawer, closet, or its original box is the perfect spot.

2. The Perfect Application

  • Optimal Number of Sprays: For a scent that comfortably lasts a minimum of 7 to 8 hours, apply 4 to 5 sprays. This is the sweet spot that ensures a strong presence without being overpowering.
  • Right Distance: Hold the bottle about 5 to 7 inches away from your skin when spraying to ensure an even mist rather than a concentrated puddle.

3. Target the Pulse Points

  • Spray directly onto your pulse points. These are areas where your veins sit closest to the skin, emitting body heat that naturally diffuses and amplifies the fragrance throughout the day.
  • Key spots: Wrists, neck, inner elbows, and right behind the ears.

4. Never Rub Your Wrists Together

  • It is a common habit to spray perfume on one wrist and rub it against the other. Do not do this.
  • Rubbing creates friction that crushes the delicate top notes of the perfume, altering the scent profile and causing it to fade much faster. Let it air dry naturally.

5. Apply on Moisturized Skin

  • Fragrance binds much better to hydrated skin. For maximum longevity, apply your perfume immediately after a shower, or apply an unscented body lotion/moisturizer to your pulse points before spraying.

6. Spraying on Clothes (Optional)

  • If you want the scent to last even longer, you can lightly mist it over your clothes. (Note: Hold the bottle slightly further away to avoid any potential staining on delicate light-colored fabrics).
